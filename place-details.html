Eiffel Tower

Champ de Mars, 5 Avenue Anatole France, 75007 Paris, France

About

The Eiffel Tower is a wrought-iron lattice tower on the Champ de Mars in Paris, France. It is named after the engineer Gustave Eiffel, whose company designed and built the tower. Locally nicknamed "La dame de fer" (French for "Iron Lady"), it was constructed from 1887 to 1889 as the entrance to the 1889 World's Fair.

It has become a global cultural icon of France and one of the most recognizable structures in the world. The tower is the tallest structure in Paris and the most-visited paid monument in the world.

History

1887

Construction Begins

Work on the foundation and first level begins on January 28, 1887.

1889

Completion

The tower is completed on March 31, 1889, just in time for the World's Fair.

1909

Permanent Structure

The tower is saved from demolition and becomes a permanent part of Paris.
